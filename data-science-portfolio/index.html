1. Final Year Project: Vehicle Health Scoring & Reporting App

The Problem

Existing OBD2 diagnostic tools provide raw fault codes (DTCs) and live data (PIDs) but lack a user-friendly, interpretive layer. The challenge was to transform this complex automotive data into an actionable, easy-to-understand scoring system and predictive health reports for vehicle owners.

The Solution

Building a comprehensive OBD2 scanning application that processes live PID and DTC data. The solution includes developing a proprietary scoring algorithm to generate a vehicle health index, along with dynamic PID and DTC progression visualizations and a stats summary that provides human-friendly interpretations of the vehicle's condition.

Impact & Key Features

  • Develops a Machine Learning/Scoring system to generate vehicle health reports per specific vehicle.
  • Creates dynamic visualizations (PID & DTC progressions) to monitor component behavior over time.
  • Key Value: Translates technical diagnostic data into clear user insights and predictive warnings.

2. Microsoft Stock Analysis (MSFT)

The Problem

Investors and analysts require clear, long-term historical context and key technical indicators to make informed decisions on a mature stock like MSFT. The challenge was to transform raw, decades-long stock data into easily interpretable visualizations and actionable financial insights.

The Solution

I conducted a comprehensive time series analysis of Microsoft stock data from the late 1980s to 2023. The solution involved Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Feature Engineering (calculating 50-Day and 200-Day Moving Averages), and creating visualizations that identify critical market events like the Dot-com bubble and the post-2016 growth phase.

Impact & Key Insights

  • Successfully engineered technical features (MA50, MA200, Daily % Change) to enable technical analysis.
  • Visualized clear Bullish trends (Price > MA50 > MA200) post-2016, confirming high-growth periods.
  • Identified strong Positive Correlations between Price and Moving Averages (0.99–1.00), and confirmed Weak/No Correlation between Price and Daily Change (a key financial insight).

Skills Demonstrated

Python | pandas | matplotlib | seaborn | Time Series Analysis | Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) | Financial Analysis | Correlation Analysis

3. Mental Health & Lifestyle Analysis

The Problem

Understanding the true correlation between highly interdependent lifestyle factors (diet, sleep, screen time) and subjective mental health outcomes (happiness, stress) is complex. The challenge was to conduct an exploratory analysis to uncover subtle, dominant patterns and quantify the relative impact of these habits on well-being.

The Solution

I performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Data Validation on a comprehensive dataset of lifestyle habits (2019–2024). The analysis utilized matplotlib and seaborn to generate visualizations comparing happiness scores across different Exercise Levels and Diet Types to isolate the most influential predictors.

Impact & Key Insights

  • Quantified Dominant Predictor: Concluded that Exercise Level is a stronger predictor of happiness than diet type.
  • Key Finding: Discovered that a High Exercise + Junk Food diet can correlate with higher happiness than a Low Exercise + Keto diet, proving the primary influence of exercise.
  • Peak Metric: Identified Vegetarian Diet + High Exercise as the combination associated with the highest average happiness score (5.68).

Skills Demonstrated

Python | pandas | matplotlib | seaborn | Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) | Data Visualization | Hypothesis Testing | Complex Correlation Analysis

4. TSLA 5-Year Stock Analysis & Predictive Modeling

The Problem

Financial decision-making in high-volatility stock markets (like TSLA) is often reactive and lacks quantitative foresight. The challenge was to create a reliable system to model future price action and identify trends that are opaque to simple daily observation.

The Solution

I conducted a comprehensive five-year time-series analysis of TSLA data, employing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and advanced Feature Engineering (including Moving Averages and Momentum Indicators). I then built a Random Forest Regressor to predict future closing prices, providing a data-driven prediction tool.

Impact & Results

  • Achieved high predictive strength with an R² score of 0.94 and RMSE of 21.46.
  • Demonstrated successful management and modeling of noisy, high-volatility financial data.

Skills Demonstrated

Python | Pandas | NumPy | Random Forest | Predictive Modeling | Financial Time-Series Analysis | Feature Engineering
